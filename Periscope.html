How a Periscope Works:

Two plane mirrors are fixed at an angle of 45° to the walls of the periscope. Light from the object hits the upper mirror at 45° and reflects down at 90°. It then hits the lower mirror at 45° and reflects horizontally into the observer's eye, allowing them to see over high obstacles.

How a Kaleidoscope Works:

A kaleidoscope uses three plane mirrors set at 60° to each other to form an equilateral triangular prism chamber. Multiple reflections occur on the mirror surfaces, multiplying the loose beads into an infinite, beautiful symmetrical pattern with 6-fold hexagonal symmetry.

Mirror Application Science

This simulation demonstrates how the physics of reflection from flat plane mirrors is used to solve engineering problems and build toys.

Key Syllabus Concepts:

  • Law of Reflection / Hukum Pantulan:
    The angle of incidence ($i$) is always equal to the angle of reflection ($r$). The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie on the same plane.
  • Periscope Path:
    By positioning two mirrors parallel to each other at 45° angles, light deviates 180° in total direction while shifting vertically, bypassing blockades.
  • Kaleidoscope Patterns:
    Three mirrors arranged at 60° generate infinitely repeating 2D patterns. Rotation shifts the loose beads, dynamically changing the optical matrix.
